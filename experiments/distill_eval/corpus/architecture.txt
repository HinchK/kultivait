PLANNING SESSION LOG — task: replace SQL LIKE search with a real search service

Turn 1: Profiled the database. The LIKE queries in core/search.py account for 31%
of total DB load at peak. This is the single biggest query family we have.
Turn 2: Surveyed options: Elasticsearch, OpenSearch, Meilisearch, Typesense, pgvector.
Turn 3: Elasticsearch evaluation — powerful but the ops burden is real: JVM tuning,
cluster management, and we have no JVM expertise on the team.
Turn 4: Long detour comparing managed Elastic pricing tiers. Ultimately irrelevant
because of the decision in turn 6.
Turn 5: Meilisearch evaluation — single binary, no JVM, typo tolerance out of the box,
and our dataset fits comfortably in one node.
Turn 6: DECISION: Meilisearch, not Elasticsearch. Rationale: our search is simple
prefix/typo matching over product listings; the ops burden of a JVM cluster is not
justified. Revisit only if we need aggregations.
Turn 7: Sized the index: 4.2M documents today, growing about 3% monthly. Single-node
Meilisearch handles this with headroom.
Turn 8: CONSTRAINT from the SRE review: search latency must stay under 50ms p95,
measured at the API layer, or the mobile team's timeout budget breaks.
Turn 9: CONSTRAINT from legal review: no PII may enter the search index. Listings
sometimes contain seller email and phone in free-text descriptions; these must be
stripped at ingestion time, not at query time.
Turn 10: Tangent into the analytics pipeline. Unrelated. Dead end.
Turn 11: Found the existing nightly job scaffold at jobs/reindex.py — it already runs
at 02:30 and is the natural home for the full-reindex pass.
Turn 12: Migration approach debated: big-bang cutover vs dual-write. Big-bang rejected
after the team recalled the tag-migration incident.
DECISION: dual-write during migration behind feature flag search_v2; reads flip
per-tenant so we can canary the two largest tenants last.
Turn 13: Checked client libraries. The official Python client is fine; no wrapper needed.
Turn 14: OPEN QUESTION: ownership after launch is unresolved — platform team or product
team owns the Meilisearch instance, its upgrades, and its pager? Must be settled in
the build phase kickoff.
Turn 15: Another tangent about renaming core/search.py. Deferred indefinitely. Dead end.
